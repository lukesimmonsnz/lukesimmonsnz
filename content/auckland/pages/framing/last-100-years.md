## Why a 100-year frame

Auckland's present-day problems — housing unaffordability, transport congestion, coastal exposure, infrastructure debt — are downstream of decisions compounded over decades. A five- or ten-year retrospective hides the structural arc. A hundred-year frame makes the arc visible: what was chosen, what was defaulted into, what was forced by population growth, geography, technology, or policy.

This page is an outline awaiting expansion. Each heading below is a thread to trace forward from roughly 1925 to the present, with citations through the entity graph the Auckland project runs on.

## Population and the Māori urban migration

The demographic arc of Auckland over a century — from roughly 200,000 in 1925 to 1.7 million today. Key turning points: the mid-century manufacturing boom drawing rural migration, the Māori urban migration of the 1950s–70s, the post-1986 liberalisation of immigration from non-Commonwealth countries. Each phase reshaped the housing demand curve and the political coalition that responded to it.

## Spatial expansion and the rise of the motorway

From a compact isthmus city of streetcars and ferries to a low-density sprawl stretching from Pukekohe to Warkworth. The decisive shifts: suburban rail retrenchment mid-century, motorway construction from the 1950s, containment under the Rural Urban Boundary from the 2016 Unitary Plan.

## Housing typology and tenure

The detached quarter-acre section as the post-war default; state housing at scale from 1937 onwards; the privatisation of state housing stock from the 1990s; the post-2016 apartment and townhouse shift. Homeownership rates across cohorts as the lagging indicator of each phase.

## Infrastructure debt and asset cycles

Water, sewerage, transport, stormwater — each built out in waves that ran ahead of growth then fell behind. The 1950s–70s build-out followed by decades of underinvestment and the present catch-up programme. The pattern is cyclical: capacity, stagnation, crisis, rebuild, capacity.

## Governance: amalgamation, regionalism, reform

The shift from dozens of local boroughs through regional council experiments to the 2010 Super City amalgamation, and the ongoing Three Waters and Resource Management Act reform debates. A century of organisational restructuring in pursuit of a working unit of decision-making for a metropolitan region.

## Climate, coast, volcano

The physical constraints that have always been there but are becoming acute: sea-level rise against Auckland's exceptionally long coastline; the Auckland Volcanic Field as a low-probability high-consequence hazard; intensifying storms demonstrated most visibly in the January 2023 floods.

## The decisions that still shape 2026

A short list of specific choices — the 1952 motorway plan, the 1996 state-housing sell-off, the 2010 amalgamation, the 2016 Unitary Plan — that did most of the work of producing the housing, transport, and governance configuration Auckland lives in now. Each is a decision a different Auckland could have made differently, with a traceable counterfactual.
