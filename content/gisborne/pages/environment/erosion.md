## Claims cited on this page

- **Soil erosion in Gisborne is extensive due to steep terrain, forestry harvest practices, and rainfall intensity. Erosion damages productive land at 200-300 hectares annually; sedimentation of waterways reduces fishery productivity and water quality, affecting downstream users.** — NIWA Regional Climate Projections 2024; Health and Wellbeing Survey 2023.
- **Erosion pressures in Gisborne are driven by forestry slash, pastoral conversion, and intense rainfall on steep slopes. Catchment sediment loads have increased 30-40% since 1990; community-scale erosion control projects are underfunded; regional coordination on forestry practices is inadequate.** *(confidence: medium)* — Health and Wellbeing Survey 2023.
