## Claims cited on this page

- **Rural crime (stock theft, farm theft, arson) across Rangitikei, Tararua, and Manawatū rural zones account for approximately 15-18% of property crime in the region. Farming families cite isolation, slow police response times, and minimal local security infrastructure as aggravating factors. Organized gang activity in rural areas has increased since 2020.** [value: 16.5 percent; 2020-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — NZ Police Crime Statistics 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Region; Rangitikei District Council Annual Report 2023.
- **Methamphetamine manufacturing and distribution has proliferated in rural Manawatū-Whanganui, particularly in Taihape, Waiouru, and isolated farm areas. Increased rural meth lab discoveries correlate with gang presence expansion and property crime spikes. Rural communities report reduced sense of safety and increased burglary targeting farm equipment and stored goods.** *(confidence: medium)* — NZ Police Crime Statistics 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Region; Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034.
