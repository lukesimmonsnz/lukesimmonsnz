## Claims cited on this page

- **Youth offending (ages 10-16) in Manawatū-Whanganui has risen approximately 12% from 2019 to 2023. Whanganui city and Palmerston North's high-deprivation suburbs account for 60-65% of youth justice referrals. Risk factors include school disengagement, intergenerational poverty, family instability, and limited youth employment pathways.** [value: 12 percent_increase_2019_2023; 2019-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — NZ Police Crime Statistics 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Region; Ministry of Education: Manawatū-Whanganui Education Performance 2023; NZ Deprivation Index 2018: Manawatū-Whanganui Data.
- **Youth gang recruitment and involvement have escalated in Manawatū-Whanganui since 2019, with gang presence in Whanganui, Palmerston North, and Waiouru. Youth programmes addressing offending (Massey University community outreach, youth employment initiatives, mentoring) remain under-funded relative to demand, with waiting lists for preventative intervention exceeding 6 months.** *(confidence: medium)* — NZ Police Crime Statistics 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Region; Massey University: Regional Research and Collaboration Platform; Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023.
