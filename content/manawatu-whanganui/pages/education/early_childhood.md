## Claims cited on this page

- **Early Childhood Education (ECE) participation in Manawatū-Whanganui averages 92-94% for ages 3-4, comparable to national levels. However, rural areas (Rangitikei, Tararua, Ruapehu) experience lower participation (86-88%) due to limited centre availability and transport barriers. Childcare costs in Palmerston North and Whanganui range NZ$65-85/day, affecting low-income family access.** [value: 91 percent_participation_3_4_years; 2023] *(confidence: medium)* — Ministry of Education: Manawatū-Whanganui Education Performance 2023; Census 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Regional Profile.
- **Whanganui city faces ECE workforce shortages; only 3-4 qualified early childhood teachers per 100 children served (below the recommended 1:6-8 ratio). Rural Manawatū-Whanganui has only 12-14 ECE services across 7 territorial authorities, creating significant gaps in Tararua, Rangitikei, and Ruapehu districts where some communities are served by single centres or none.** *(confidence: medium)* — Ministry of Education: Manawatū-Whanganui Education Performance 2023; Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023.
