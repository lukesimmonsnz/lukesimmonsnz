## Claims cited on this page

- **Secondary schools in Manawatū-Whanganui serve approximately 28,000 students across 42 schools. Palmerston North (Palmerston North Boys' High, Palmerston North Girls' High) and Whanganui (Whanganui High School) are the region's largest secondary schools (each approx. 800-1,000 students). Rural areas have smaller, sometimes combined schools (e.g., Taihape Area School, Ohakune Community College) with limited subject choices and specialist services.** [value: 28000 secondary_students; 2023] *(confidence: medium)* — Ministry of Education: Manawatū-Whanganui Education Performance 2023; Census 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Regional Profile.
- **School disengagement and truancy rates in Manawatū-Whanganui are elevated in high-deprivation areas. Whanganui reports 18-22% chronic absenteeism (>50 days/year absent), compared to 8-10% in Palmerston North and 10-15% rural. Secondary retention to Year 13 is 71-75% regionally, below the national average of 78-80%. Socioeconomic stress and limited post-secondary employment pathways drive early school leaving.** [value: 16 percent_average_chronic_absenteeism; 2022-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — Ministry of Education: Manawatū-Whanganui Education Performance 2023; NZ Deprivation Index 2018: Manawatū-Whanganui Data.
