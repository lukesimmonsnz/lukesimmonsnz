## Claims cited on this page

- **Manawatū River water quality has deteriorated 40-50% over the past 30 years due to agricultural intensification and urban runoff. Sediment yield from the catchment exceeds 400 tonnes/km2/year, among NZ's highest. Dissolved oxygen levels drop below 4 mg/L in summer months (hypoxia threshold), limiting native fish habitat. Horizons' target: restore nitrate to <10 mg/L by 2050, requiring significant farm management changes.** [value: 425 tonnes_per_km2_per_year_sediment; 2000-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — State of the Manawatū River: Water Quality Assessment 2023; One Plan: Horizons Regional Council Integrated Strategy.
- **E. coli contamination in the Manawatū River varies seasonally but frequently exceeds safe recreation thresholds (260 CFU/100mL guideline). Urban treatment plants in Palmerston North and Feilding contribute to point-source pollution; non-point agricultural runoff dominates. Maori health advisories and recreational swimming bans are common in summer. River restoration projects (riparian planting, constructed wetlands) involve Horizons, Rangitāne iwi, and local farmers but cover <10% of the catchment.** *(confidence: medium)* — State of the Manawatū River: Water Quality Assessment 2023; Rangitāne o Tamaki Nui-a-Rua: Iwi Management Plan 2022.
