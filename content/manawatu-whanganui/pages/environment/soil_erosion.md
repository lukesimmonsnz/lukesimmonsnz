## Claims cited on this page

- **Soil erosion in Manawatū-Whanganui affects approximately 15-18% of productive farmland, particularly in hill-country areas (Tararua, Ruapehu, Rangitikei). Intensive dairy grazing, winter pugging (animal hoof damage), and deforestation of native bush have accelerated erosion and sediment loss. Eroded soil reduces farm productivity and contributes to river siltation. Conservation regimes (contour planting, riparian buffers) are adopted by approx. 25-30% of affected farmers.** [value: 16.5 percent_farmland_affected; 2020-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — Horizons Regional Council Data and Research Services; One Plan: Horizons Regional Council Integrated Strategy.
- **Soil carbon stocks in Manawatū-Whanganui agricultural soils have declined approximately 20-25% since 1990 due to intensive tillage, monoculture cropping, and reduced organic matter inputs. This loss reduces soil health, productivity, and carbon sequestration capacity. Regenerative agriculture adoption (cover crops, minimal tillage, rotational grazing) has increased from approx. 5% (2015) to approx. 15% (2024), but scaling remains slow due to cost and knowledge barriers.** [value: 22.5 percent_decline_soil_carbon_since_1990; 1990-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — Horizons Regional Council Data and Research Services; Treasury Economic Outlook 2024: Regional Analysis.
