## Claims cited on this page

- **Manawatū-Whanganui has lost approximately 85-90% of its pre-European wetland area (from approx. 18,000 hectares to approx. 2,000 hectares). Drainage for agriculture, urban development, and transport infrastructure (SH1, rail) destroyed critical wetland habitat. Remaining wetlands are fragmented and degraded. The region retains only 2-3 major intact wetland systems (Himatangi Beach, Upper Manawatū, Whanganui estuary), all threatened by nutrient pollution and climate impacts.** [value: 87.5 percent_wetland_loss_pre_european; 1800-2024] — Horizons Regional Council Data and Research Services; One Plan: Horizons Regional Council Integrated Strategy.
- **Wetland restoration initiatives in Manawatū-Whanganui cover approximately 200-250 hectares (organised through Horizons, DOC, and community groups), but restoration is slow (10-15 hectares/year) relative to the scale of loss. Funding for wetland projects is constrained. Long-term degradation of remaining wetlands from nutrient loading (agricultural runoff, point-source discharge) threatens aquatic biodiversity and migratory bird populations (shoveller, grey teal). Invasive species (crack willow, raupo monoculture) further reduce ecological value.** *(confidence: medium)* — One Plan: Horizons Regional Council Integrated Strategy; Horizons Regional Council Data and Research Services.
