## Claims cited on this page

- **The Whanganui River was granted legal personhood in 2017 under the Te Awa Tupua Act, becoming the world's first river with this legal status. Whanganui Iwi hold joint stewardship with Crown. The river has its own governance board and dedicated funding for restoration. This precedent recognizes Māori cultural and spiritual values and has influenced freshwater governance nationally. Wider implementation of iwi co-governance in Manawatū-Whanganui remains uneven; Rangitāne involvement in Manawatū River management is growing but formal structures are still developing.** — Rangitāne o Tamaki Nui-a-Rua: Iwi Management Plan 2022; Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023.
