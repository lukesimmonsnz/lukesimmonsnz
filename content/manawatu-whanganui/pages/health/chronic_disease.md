## Claims cited on this page

- **Chronic diseases (type 2 diabetes, hypertension, cardiovascular disease) account for approximately 65-70% of mortality and 60-65% of disability in Manawatū-Whanganui. Diabetes prevalence is approximately 8-10% of the population, above national average (7-8%). Whanganui city and rural areas have higher prevalence and mortality rates than Palmerston North. Risk factors include high rates of obesity (approx. 35-38% of adults), smoking (18-22%), and sedentary lifestyles, correlating with socioeconomic deprivation.** [value: 67.5 percent_mortality_chronic_disease; 2021-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — Health NZ: Manawatū-Whanganui Population Health Profile 2023; NZ Deprivation Index 2018: Manawatū-Whanganui Data.
- **Cardiovascular disease mortality in Manawatū-Whanganui is 18-22% above the national average, driven by high prevalence of risk factors (hypertension, high cholesterol, obesity, smoking). Whanganui city district records the highest district-level cardiovascular mortality rate in the region. Access to cardiac rehabilitation and preventive services is limited in rural areas (Tararua, Rangitikei, Ruapehu); patients often travel to Palmerston North or Wellington for specialist cardiology and interventional procedures.** [value: 20 percent_above_national_average; 2020-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — Health NZ: Manawatū-Whanganui Population Health Profile 2023.
