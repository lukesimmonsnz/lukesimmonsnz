## Claims cited on this page

- **Mental health and psychological distress affect approximately 15-18% of the adult population in Manawatū-Whanganui, with anxiety and depression being the most prevalent conditions. Whanganui city and rural areas record higher rates of mental health service utilization and hospital admissions for mental health crisis than Palmerston North. Suicide rate in the region is approximately 13-15 per 100,000 per year, slightly above national average (12-13), with elevated rates in rural areas (Rangitikei, Tararua, Ruapehu).** [value: 16.5 percent_adult_psychological_distress; 2021-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — Health NZ: Manawatū-Whanganui Population Health Profile 2023.
- **Mental health service capacity in Manawatū-Whanganui is constrained; approximately 25-30% of people seeking support face waitlists exceeding 3 months. Rural areas (Tararua, Ruapehu, parts of Rangitikei) have minimal local mental health services, forcing patients to travel to Palmerston North or Whanganui. Community mental health teams operate in major towns but are under-resourced. Peer support and iwi-led mental health services (e.g., Rangitāne kaupapa Māori health) provide crucial gaps but remain underfunded relative to need.** *(confidence: medium)* — Health NZ: Manawatū-Whanganui Population Health Profile 2023.
