## Claims cited on this page

- **Rural health workforce shortages affect Manawatū-Whanganui significantly. Approximately 40-50% of rural general practices lack a full-time GP; locum cover is common. Rural areas (Tararua, Rangitikei, Ruapehu, parts of Horowhenua) have nurse-led clinics, aged care gaps, and limited allied health (physiotherapy, psychology, dentistry) services. Recruitment and retention of rural health professionals is challenging; remuneration and lifestyle factors drive migration to urban centres. Health NZ is piloting rural workforce development programmes (2023-2024) to improve rural staffing.** *(confidence: medium)* — Health NZ: Manawatū-Whanganui Population Health Profile 2023.
- **Aged care workforce in Manawatū-Whanganui faces critical shortages as the population ages. Approximately 18-22% of the region's population is aged 65+; rural districts (Ruapehu, Rangitikei, Tararua) have even higher proportions (25-28%). Residential aged care facilities operate at capacity; home and community care provider networks are fragmented and underfunded. Rural areas struggle to attract care workers due to low wages, poor conditions, and limited housing. Recruitment costs and staff turnover (25-30% annually) strain aged care budgets.** *(confidence: medium)* — Health NZ: Manawatū-Whanganui Population Health Profile 2023; Census 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Regional Profile.
