## Claims cited on this page

- **Manawatū-Whanganui's housing market is bifurcated: Palmerston North median house prices increased from NZ$400,000 (2019) to NZ$520,000 (2024), reflecting strong demand from Massey students and urban migrants. Whanganui and rural areas saw more modest gains (NZ$250,000 to NZ$310,000), with lower investor demand. Owner-occupancy rates are 65-68% regionally, below the national average (70%+). Rental vacancy is tight (2-3%); rental affordability is under stress in Palmerston North, with median rents at 35-40% of median household income.** [value: 415 thousand_nzd_median_house_price; 2024] *(confidence: medium)* — Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034; Housing Aotearoa 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Chapter.
