## Claims cited on this page

- **Palmerston North city is experiencing rapid population growth (approximately 2.5-3% per annum), driven by Massey University expansion (student intake target: 6,500), logistics sector growth, and urban migration. Housing demand significantly outpaces supply; new residential zoning under the Spatial Plan (2024) targets 4,000-5,000 new dwellings over 10 years. Construction is concentrated in Kelvin Grove, Highbury, and eastern suburbs, but affordable housing shortage persists; first-home buyer affordability has worsened substantially (median price-to-income ratio now 8.5x, up from 6.2x in 2018).** [value: 2.75 percent_annual_growth; 2019-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034; Housing Aotearoa 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Chapter.
- **Palmerston North's rental market is strained; student housing demand from Massey University drives competition for limited supply. Council-funded affordable housing initiatives (approximately 150-200 social housing units planned 2024-2028) aim to address needs of low-income and vulnerable households. Private rental stock averaged NZ$420-480/week (1-2 bedroom units) in 2024, up 30-35% from 2019. Rental affordability is most acute for families earning <NZ$50,000 p.a. and beneficiaries; ratios exceed 50% of household income.** *(confidence: medium)* — Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034; Housing Aotearoa 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Chapter.
