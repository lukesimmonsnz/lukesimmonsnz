## Claims cited on this page

- **Rural housing in Manawatū-Whanganui is often in poor condition or owner-lacking maintenance investment. Approximately 12-15% of rural dwellings (particularly in Rangitikei, Tararua, Ruapehu) are substandard or lack adequate water, heating, or sanitation. Farm viability pressures (climatic stress, commodity price volatility) constrain household income for housing maintenance and improvements. Rural housing availability for agricultural workers and service professionals is constrained; limited new construction in small towns (Taihape, Ohakune, Levin) restricts options.** [value: 13.5 percent_substandard_rural_housing; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — Housing Aotearoa 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Chapter; NZ Deprivation Index 2018: Manawatū-Whanganui Data.
- **Rural housing in Manawatū-Whanganui faces climate and infrastructure challenges. Flooding risk (Manawatū River, tributary overtopping) affects properties in Foxton, Levin, and scattered rural homes; flood insurance has become prohibitively expensive or unavailable. Rural broadband and mobile coverage gaps affect housing attractiveness; high-speed internet access is available in <40% of rural properties, limiting remote work options for young families. Wastewater and water supply infrastructure in small communities is aging; capacity for new development is constrained by network limitations.** *(confidence: medium)* — Housing Aotearoa 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Chapter; NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023.
