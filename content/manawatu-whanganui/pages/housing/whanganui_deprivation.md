## Claims cited on this page

- **Whanganui city has one of the highest housing deprivation rates in New Zealand (NZ Deprivation Index Quintile 5, most deprived). Approximately 30-35% of households live in housing classified as overcrowded, unheated, damp, or lacking access to adequate washing facilities. Public housing stock managed by Housing New Zealand / Kāinga Ora covers approximately 8-10% of the city's population, but demand far exceeds supply. Private rental stock is concentrated in older, low-quality buildings; median rents are lower than Palmerston North (NZ$300-350/week) but represent 45-50% of median household income due to low incomes.** [value: 32.5 percent_housing_deprivation; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023; NZ Deprivation Index 2018: Manawatū-Whanganui Data.
- **Whanganui city council is implementing a housing revival strategy (2024-2034) targeting 500-600 new residential dwellings, renovation of existing housing stock, and incentives for residential conversion of vacant commercial buildings. Heritage preservation overlays and inner-city revitalization aim to improve housing quality and affordability. However, funding constraints, low development returns, and population decline (Whanganui population has declined approx. 3% since 2013) limit private investment. Council-led and community initiatives (Ngāti Toa Rangatira housing co-ops, Whanganui iwi housing partnerships) are expanding but remain under-resourced.** *(confidence: medium)* — Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023; Housing Aotearoa 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Chapter.
