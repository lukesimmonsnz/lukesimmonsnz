## Claims cited on this page

- **Child poverty (0-17 years) in Manawatū-Whanganui affects approximately 20-24% of children, above the national average (approx. 17-19%). Whanganui city has the highest rate (28-32%), followed by rural areas with dispersed poverty. Child poverty correlates strongly with parental unemployment, single-parent households, and benefit dependency. Food insecurity, educational underachievement, and health disparities (dental neglect, respiratory illness, obesity) are associated. School holiday and weekend meal programmes operate in Whanganui and Palmerston North disadvantaged zones to address gaps.** [value: 22 percent_child_poverty; 2021-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — NZ Deprivation Index 2018: Manawatū-Whanganui Data; Census 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Regional Profile.
- **Child nutrition support in Manawatū-Whanganui is provided through school lunch programmes (Whanganui District Schools, Palmerston North East End schools) and community food banks, but coverage is patchy. Approximately 35-40% of children in high-deprivation areas experience food insecurity (skipping meals, reduced portion sizes, poor diet quality). Cost-of-living crisis (2022-2024) has exacerbated childhood nutritional vulnerability. Community organisations (Feeding our Whānau, Gleaning networks, iwi-led food security projects) provide supplementary support but are under-resourced relative to demand.** *(confidence: medium)* — Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023; Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034.
