## Claims cited on this page

- **Rural isolation in Manawatū-Whanganui affects approximately 15,000-18,000 people living in dispersed settlements (Taihape, Ohakune, Raurimu, Taumarunui, Waiouru, rural Rangitikei). Limited public transport, high vehicle dependency, and geographic distance from services create structural isolation. Rural youth (ages 15-24) face limited employment and education opportunities; out-migration to Palmerston North or Wellington is significant (net outflow approx. 2-3% p.a.). Broadband gaps and mobile coverage dead zones exacerbate isolation by limiting digital access to employment, education, and social connection.** [value: 16500 people_in_rural_isolation; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — Census 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Regional Profile; NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023.
- **Rural service access in Manawatū-Whanganui is constrained. Primary healthcare, secondary education, and specialist services concentrate in Palmerston North and Whanganui; rural areas require travel (30-90 minutes) for non-routine care. Rural schools are small (Taihape Area School, Ohakune Community College) with limited subject choice and staff; secondary students travel or board in larger towns. Community transport services (dial-a-ride, volunteer networks) operate sporadically; funding is insecure. Older people and those without vehicle access face compounding isolation and delayed healthcare access.** *(confidence: medium)* — Ministry of Education: Manawatū-Whanganui Education Performance 2023; Health NZ: Manawatū-Whanganui Population Health Profile 2023.
