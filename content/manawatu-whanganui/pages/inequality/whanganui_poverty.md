## Claims cited on this page

- **Whanganui city has a poverty rate (below 60% of median household income) of approximately 32-36%, among the highest in New Zealand. Median household income is approximately NZ$48,000 (versus national median NZ$58,000). Benefit dependency is high (approximately 18-22% of working-age population on main benefits, vs national 12-14%). Long-term unemployment and underemployment are persistent; unemployment rate in Whanganui averages 6-7%, versus Palmerston North 4-5%. Youth unemployment is even higher (12-15%), contributing to intergenerational poverty and limited economic mobility.** [value: 34 percent_poverty_rate; 2021-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — Census 2023: Manawatū-Whanganui Regional Profile; NZ Deprivation Index 2018: Manawatū-Whanganui Data.
- **Whanganui's economy struggles to generate sufficient employment; dominant sectors (arts/culture, tourism, small retail) offer limited full-time, well-paid positions. Manufacturing and processing jobs have declined significantly (2010-2024). Benefit-to-employment transitions are slow; approximately 30-40% of working-age benefit recipients are classified as long-term jobseekers. Wage growth and job quality lag regional averages. Community employment initiatives (NZDF Waiouru proximity, heritage tourism, arts economy) are under-developed. Poverty reduction requires sustained multi-sector investment (education, mental health, employment support, housing) beyond current funding levels.** *(confidence: medium)* — Treasury Economic Outlook 2024: Regional Analysis; Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023.
