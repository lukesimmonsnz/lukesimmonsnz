## Claims cited on this page

- **Rural digital infrastructure in Manawatū-Whanganui is fragmented. Broadband availability (10 Mbps or higher) covers approximately 65-70% of rural areas; the remaining 30-35% have unreliable 4G mobile or satellite-only options. Deployment costs for fibre and fixed wireless access are high in dispersed communities; uptake has been slower than urban areas. The National Broadband Initiative (2019-2028) targets 99%+ coverage, but rural implementation lags. Digital divide disadvantages rural businesses (agritech adoption, precision farming), remote workers, and education access.** [value: 67.5 percent_rural_broadband_adequate; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023; Horizons Regional Council Data and Research Services.
- **Mobile coverage gaps persist in rural Manawatū-Whanganui; approximately 15-20% of rural land area lacks reliable cellular (3G or higher) coverage, particularly in Tararua, Rangitikei, and parts of Ruapehu. Farms, small communities (Taihape, Ohakune, Taumarunui), and state highways have dead zones causing emergency communication delays. Telecommunications companies cite low population density and profitability challenges as barriers to investment. Community-led and council-funded tower projects are emerging but are slow and underfunded. Digital access constraints limit agri-tech adoption, remote healthcare, and youth employment in rural areas.** [value: 17.5 percent_land_area_no_mobile; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023; Horizons Regional Council Data and Research Services.
