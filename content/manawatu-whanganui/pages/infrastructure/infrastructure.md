## Claims cited on this page

- **Manawatū-Whanganui's core infrastructure (roading, water, wastewater, energy) faces significant renewal demands. Many rural water schemes operate with aging assets (30-50 years old); pipe breaks and water loss are common. Palmerston North's stormwater network is near capacity; climate change (increased rainfall intensity) threatens flooding. Wastewater treatment plants in regional towns (Whanganui, Feilding, Levin) require upgrades. Energy transition (local renewable generation, EV charging infrastructure) is beginning but coordination is weak. Council budgets are constrained; infrastructure funding gaps total approximately NZ$300-400 million over the next 10 years.** [value: 350 million_nzd_estimated_funding_gap; 2024-2034] *(confidence: medium)* — Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034; Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023.
