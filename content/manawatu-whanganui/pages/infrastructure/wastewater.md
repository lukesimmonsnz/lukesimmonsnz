## Claims cited on this page

- **Wastewater treatment in Manawatū-Whanganui is managed by territorial authorities; systems vary from modern treatment plants (Palmerston North) to aging systems (Whanganui, small rural towns). Palmerston North's treatment plant (Riverside) processes approx. 35,000 m3/day; compliance with environmental discharge standards is generally met but margins are tight during wet weather. Whanganui's plant operates at 70-75% capacity; upgrades are planned (2025-2030). Rural schemes often use on-site or small community systems; approximately 20-25% of rural properties rely on septic tanks or poorly maintained systems, with occasional contamination of groundwater.** *(confidence: medium)* — Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034; Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023.
- **Wastewater nutrient discharge (nitrogen, phosphorus) from treatment plants contributes to Manawatū River and tributary degradation. Palmerston North's plant discharges approximately 50-80 tonnes of nitrogen annually. Whanganui plant discharge similarly contributes to downstream impacts. Three Waters Reform (2024 proposal) seeks to centralize wastewater governance and increase investment in nutrient-removal upgrades. Costs are substantial (estimated NZ$150-200 million across the region); council budgets cannot sustain this alone. Central government co-funding is essential to meet environmental standards and support river restoration goals.** *(confidence: medium)* — Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034; One Plan: Horizons Regional Council Integrated Strategy.
