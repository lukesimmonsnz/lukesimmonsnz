## Claims cited on this page

- **Urban water supply in Manawatū-Whanganui relies on river abstraction (Manawatū River for Palmerston North, Whanganui River for Whanganui city) and some groundwater. Palmerston North abstracts approximately 45,000-50,000 m3/day; demand peaks at approx. 55,000 m3/day in summer. Water losses through pipe leakage are approximately 18-22% (above national average 15%). Storage reservoirs are limited; drought risk under climate change is significant. Water pricing has increased (average household now NZ$800-900/year), affecting affordability for low-income households. Horizons' One Plan includes water demand management and efficiency targets.** *(confidence: medium)* — Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034; One Plan: Horizons Regional Council Integrated Strategy.
- **Rural water supply in Manawatū-Whanganui is highly fragmented; approximately 45-50% of rural communities rely on small local schemes with limited treatment and storage. These systems are vulnerable to contamination (boil-water notices are common) and drought. Upgrade and consolidation of rural water schemes is a priority; estimated investment is NZ$80-120 million over 10 years. Whanganui District and smaller councils are coordinating scheme upgrades with central government support (Three Waters Reform). Challenges include funding, affordability for rural users, and water availability under climate stress in hill-country areas.** *(confidence: medium)* — Whanganui City Council Annual Report 2023; One Plan: Horizons Regional Council Integrated Strategy.
