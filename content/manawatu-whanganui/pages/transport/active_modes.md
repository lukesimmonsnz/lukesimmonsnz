## Claims cited on this page

- **Active transport (walking, cycling) usage in Manawatū-Whanganui is limited; approximately 8-10% of commute trips are active (below national 12-15%). Palmerston North has invested in cycle lanes and pedestrian infrastructure; the city targets 12-15% mode share by 2030. Whanganui and rural areas have minimal cycle infrastructure. Weather (high rainfall, wind), terrain, and distance to work/school limit active mode adoption. Equity gaps exist: lower-income households are more likely to walk but face unsafe conditions; children's independent mobility is constrained by traffic speeds and road design. Cycling and walking initiatives focus on school routes and urban centres.** [value: 9 percent_active_mode_commute; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023; Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034.
- **Safe routes to schools initiative in Manawatū-Whanganui has improved walking and cycling infrastructure around 8-10 primary and secondary schools in Palmerston North and Whanganui. Traffic calming, crossing improvements, and footpath repairs have enabled modest increases in child active transport (4% to 6% of school trips). However, rural school students remain car-dependent; school bus services are limited and require long travel times. Winter weather and darkness (June-August) further suppress active mode use. Sustained investment in infrastructure maintenance and behaviour change campaigns is needed to increase active transport contribution to regional carbon reduction and health goals.** *(confidence: medium)* — NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023.
