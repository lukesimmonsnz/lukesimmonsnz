## Claims cited on this page

- **Palmerston North experiences increasing traffic congestion, particularly at peak hours (7-9 am, 4-6 pm) on main arterials (SH1 northbound/southbound, Range Road, Church Street). Congestion at the Freyberg Road/SH1 intersection averages 8-12 minute delays during peak hours; freight routing adds to congestion. Population growth (2.5-3% p.a.) is outpacing road capacity and public transport. Traffic modelling suggests congestion will worsen 20-30% by 2035 without intervention (increased PT, demand management, roading upgrades). Freight volumes are projected to grow 25-30% over the next decade as Palmerston North's logistics hub role expands.** [value: 10 minute_average_peak_delay; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023; Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034.
- **Palmerston North's public transport system operates 12-15 urban bus routes with average ridership of approx. 1,200-1,500 passengers/day (approx. 3-4% mode share). Frequency is limited (30-60 minute headways on most routes); reliability is moderate (80-85% on-time performance). Bus network coverage is reasonable in central and western suburbs but east and north suburbs have limited service. Student transport (Massey commuting) uses informal carpooling and parking; university shuttle services cover campus but not wider city integration. Bus patronage growth has been flat (2019-2024) despite population growth, suggesting insufficient service quality/frequency to attract users from cars. NZTA/Council studies (2024) propose frequency increases and network redesign to improve mode share to 8-10% by 2035.** [value: 3.5 percent_bus_mode_share; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023; Palmerston North City Council Long-term Plan 2024-2034.
