## Claims cited on this page

- **Regional roading in Manawatū-Whanganui is aging; approximately 35-40% of local roads are in fair or poor condition (NZTA classification). Rural roads (Rangitikei, Tararua, Ruapehu) experience higher maintenance costs due to topography, weather exposure, and isolated communities. Road user charges and fuel excise tax generate insufficient revenue to meet renewal demand; councils face maintenance backlogs (estimated 5-7 years) and deferred upgrades. Heavy freight volumes (dairy, sheep, grain) accelerate pavement wear. Intersection safety on SH1 and rural state highways remains a concern; fatal and serious injury crashes average 12-15 per year regionally, above per-capita national rates.** [value: 37.5 percent_road_fair_or_poor; 2023-2024] *(confidence: medium)* — NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023; Rangitikei District Council Annual Report 2023.
- **Rural road safety in Manawatū-Whanganui reflects risk factors including high speeds, poor visibility (curves, weather), limited passing lanes, and driver fatigue (long commutes). SH3 (Ohakune to Waiouru section), SH1 (Rangitikei section), and Tararua rural roads experience elevated crash rates. Māori and rural community death rates from transport accidents are significantly elevated; approximately 18-22% of regional transport fatalities involve Māori, despite representing approx. 10-12% of regional population. Speed management, road maintenance, and driver education initiatives (Vision Zero programs) are underway but underfunded relative to scale of problem. Terrain and weather (alpine passes, winter snow/ice) make some routes inherently high-risk.** [value: 20 percent_fatalities_maori; 2020-2023] *(confidence: medium)* — NZTA: Manawatū-Whanganui Transport Strategy 2023.
