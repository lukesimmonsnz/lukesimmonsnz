## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Iwi-led economic development and Treaty settlements

Treaty settlements and iwi investment arms are the most effective mechanism for rebuilding Māori economic participation in Northland.

**Flagship moves:**

- Progress remaining Treaty settlements with Te Tai Tokerau iwi
- Direct Crown investment to iwi-owned enterprise and land development
- Fund Māori housing development on papakāinga land

**Tensions:**

- Settlement pace is constrained by Crown fiscal limits
- Benefits of iwi investment take decades to reach whānau level
- Internal iwi governance challenges affect delivery

**Interventions on the system:**

- Progress remaining Treaty settlements with Te Tai Tokerau iwi (state variable: `inequality_pressure_index`, sign: +)


### Targeted income and social support expansion

Expanding income support, social services, and regional development funding reduces the immediate effects of poverty.

**Flagship moves:**

- Increase Working for Families thresholds for regional cost of living
- Expand social housing in Whangārei and Kaitāia
- Fund community social workers in high-deprivation areas

**Tensions:**

- Transfer payments reduce poverty but do not build economic capacity
- Service access in rural areas remains constrained
- Political resistance to regionally differentiated welfare settings

**Interventions on the system:**

- Increase Working for Families thresholds for regional cost of living (state variable: `inequality_pressure_index`, sign: +)

## Claims cited on this page

- **Rural Far North faces extreme service isolation and deprivation. Towns like Rawene, Kawakawa, Te Hapua, Mitimiti lack access to tertiary healthcare, supermarkets, banking, and reliable public transport. Commuting to Whangārei or Auckland costs time and money; essential journey burdens fall heaviest on low-income whānau. Rural property ownership costs (maintenance, rates, insurance) suppress household savings.** *(confidence: medium)* — Northland Regional Council State of the Environment Report 2023.
- **Agricultural employment in Far North (dairy, horticulture, sheep/beef) offers seasonal, low-wage, precarious work; farm labour often under-documented and under-paid. Employment in primary sectors does not offer pathways to skill development or career progression; workers trapped in low-income cycles. Rural unemployment follows seasonal patterns; winter months see income collapse for seasonal workers.** *(confidence: medium)* — Northland Regional Council State of the Environment Report 2023.
