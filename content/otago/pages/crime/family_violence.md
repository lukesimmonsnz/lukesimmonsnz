### Family Violence Prevention and Support

Integrated prevention (education, therapy), perpetrator accountability, and survivor support reduce family violence.

**Flagship moves:**

- Expand family violence services capacity
- Implement universal relationship and consent education in schools
- Strengthen perpetrator accountability and rehabilitation programs

**Tensions:**

- Cultural change requirement; resource intensity

## Claims cited on this page

- **Family violence call rates in rural Otago (Clutha, Waitaki districts) are elevated; geographic isolation and limited victim service access (Balclutha, Oamaru, small towns 60-150km from Dunedin support services) compound response challenges. Police Family Harm data shows rates in South Otago ~15-20% above national average; housing instability and economic stress in farming/rural communities contribute.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
- **Youth offending in Otago is concentrated in Dunedin (student/young adult demographics) and Queenstown (visitor and seasonal worker town attracting 18-35 age cohort). North Dunedin flat-party culture and Queenstown hospitality worker population drive assaults and disorder; rural youth offending lower but property crime (theft from farms, vehicles) present in Clutha/Waitaki.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
- **Family violence presentation is increasing in rural Otago: Clutha district (Balclutha, surrounding farming area) and Waitaki (Oamaru, Palmerston) showing upward 3-year trend; service access gaps worsening as rural GPs and nurses retire; alcohol and economic stress correlated with incident frequency; prevention funding gaps in small towns.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
- **Safety outcomes in Otago show regional variation: Dunedin urban (North Dunedin student precinct) concentrates flat/party-related property crime; rural areas (Clutha, Waitaki) see farm theft (vehicles, fuel, stock) and isolation-exacerbated FV; Queenstown CBD bar-district assault prevalence notable; visitor-inflated seasonal peaks create policing resource strain.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
- **Public safety is bifurcated: Queenstown visitor-season crime spiking with tourism recovery; Dunedin student offending following academic calendar (O-Week peaks, summer dips); rural property crime steady-to-rising as farm equipment values increase and isolated rural community cohesion weakens; overall South Island per-capita crime rates remain below national mean but trend direction mixed by locality.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
- **Queenstown (2M+ annual visitors pre-COVID, recovering to 80-90% by 2023) experiences alcohol-related offending concentrated in CBD bar precinct (Mall Street, Cow Lane, suburban hospitality clusters). Visitor-related assault, drink-driving, and disorder calls represent ~25-30% of Police dispatch volume in summer months; seasonal worker population (25,000+) creates volatile demand environments.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
- **Crime trends in Queenstown are seasonal and volatile: visitor season (Dec-Mar) sees 3-4× increase in liquor-fuelled offending vs winter; bar closures and hospitality industry contraction 2020-2021 reduced incidents, recovery 2023-2024 reversing that trend; RSE and seasonal worker housing pressure (Arrowtown, Cromwell, Frankton) creating satellite crime foci.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
- **Youth crime trends show strong seasonal and demographic patterns: Dunedin peaks at O-Week (March, return to academic year) and university breaks; Queenstown volatile with visitor season; rural youth offending (Balclutha, Oamaru) low volume, property-focused (farm machinery theft). National youth offending targets being met unevenly; Otago pockets of concern in university and tourism epicentres.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
