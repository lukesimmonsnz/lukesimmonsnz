- **Economic structure is consolidating: Queenstown tourism recovering but employment increasingly 'gig' / seasonal (RSE, hospitality); wine region facing climate/water stress; dairy and sheep-beef commodity exposure volatile; University of Otago and Otago Polytechnic stable but constrained by tertiary funding; Dunedin Hospital rebuild ($2.3B+) a multi-year economic stimulus but not structural growth.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
- **Queenstown-Lakes tourism is a dominant economic force: 2M+ annual visitors (pre-COVID, recovering); adventure sector (bungy, jet-boat, heli-skiing), lake/mountain recreation, wine trail (Central Otago), and hospitality generate $2.5-3B spend. Visitor industry employs 25,000+ directly and indirectly; accommodation shortage (3,500+ Airbnb listings competing with long-term stock) and worker housing crisis endemic. - **Otago's economy is tri-partite: (1) Tourism anchored in Queenstown-Lakes District (~$2.5-3B visitor spend pre-COVID, 80-90% recovered 2023); (2) Wine + stone fruit agri-export (Central Otago Pinot Noir $180M+ export, cherries/apricots major China market); (3) Tertiary institutions (University of Otago ~$1.5B GDP contribution, Otago Polytechnic trades/health), health services (Dunedin Hospital regional hub), and Clutha dairy (Fonterra Edendale supply). Structural dependency on tourism and education creates economic volatility.** *(confidence: medium)* — Otago Regional Council Statistics 2024.
