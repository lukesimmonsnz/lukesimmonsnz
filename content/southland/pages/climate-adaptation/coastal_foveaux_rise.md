- **Agricultural drought events in Southland are projected to increase in frequency and severity. Climate models suggest dry summer events shifting from 1-in-10 year to 1-in-5 year recurrence. - **Flood risk from rainfall events and river overflow affects Southland's main rivers (Mataura, Oreti, Waiau). 1-in-100-year flood depths are projected to increase 0.3-0.6m by 2100; rural communities and state highways face inundation during extreme events.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
