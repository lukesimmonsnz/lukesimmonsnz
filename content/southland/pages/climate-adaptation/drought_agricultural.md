Drought frequency in eastern Southland increasing (dry summers now 1-in-5 year rather than 1-in-10 year); Fiordland rainfall variability increasing; coastal sea-level rise acceleration (0.5-1.2m by 2100 RCP 6.0) combined with storm surge intensification. Adaptation investments (flood defences, irrigation infrastructure, coastal realignment) face significant funding constraints.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Foveaux Strait coastal erosion is progressive, with recession rates of 0.3-0.5 metres annually in some locations. Climate-driven sea-level rise and increased storminess accelerate erosion; engineering solutions (seawalls) are costly ($100-200M); nature-based adaptation is limited by available space.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Agricultural drought events in Southland are projected to increase in frequency and severity. Climate models suggest dry summer events shifting from 1-in-10 year to 1-in-5 year recurrence. Irrigation development on Southland Plains limited by Waiau and Oreti River water availability constraints; farm-level adaptation (fodder storage, supplementary irrigation) requires capital investment.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Flood risk from rainfall events and river overflow affects Southland's main rivers (Mataura, Oreti, Waiau). 1-in-100-year flood depths are projected to increase 0.3-0.6m by 2100; rural communities and state highways face inundation during extreme events.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.

- **Southland dairy and pastoral farming face increasing drought risk. Eastern Southland (Mossburn, Lumsden, Riversdale) experiences episodic dry summers (2-3 month soil moisture deficits). Fonterra Edendale milk supply vulnerable; pastoral livestock survival affected.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
