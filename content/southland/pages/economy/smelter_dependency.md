No alternative large-scale industrial base; downstream processing (freezing works, dairy) dependent on agricultural input supply.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Southland's economy is narrowly dependent on primary industries. Dairy (Fonterra Edendale, Mataura Valley Milk), pastoral farming, and Tiwai Point Aluminium Smelter collectively represent >65% of regional employment. Limited secondary/tertiary sectors; tourism (Fiordland) is underdeveloped relative to pre-COVID potential. Manufacturing and professional services underrepresented.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Industrial transition prospects in Southland remain contingent on smelter longevity. Power supply contract extends to end of 2026; no post-smelter industrial strategy published. Regional development focus remains agricultural productivity; value-add processing limited; renewable energy export potential unexplored relative to capability.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Southland's economic dependency on Tiwai Point Aluminium Smelter is substantial and entrenched. NZAS operates 850 MW Manapouri hydroelectric station supply contract; smelter represents ~1,000 direct jobs (~1% regional employment) and ~2,000 indirect jobs in logistics, power generation, and services. Rio Tinto (79.4%) + Sumitomo (20.6%) ownership; strategic closure decisions made offshore.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Smelter dependency creates regional economic vulnerability. Power contract extended to end 2026 (then under review); no long-term commitment beyond this date. Closure would trigger ~3,000 job loss, regional output contraction >8%, multiplier effects on retail/services employment. Regional economic development strategy absent; diversification efforts underfunded relative to smelter transition risk.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Economic diversification in Southland faces structural constraints. Smelter power contract extended to 2026 (subject to review); dairy commodity prices volatile; tourism recovery post-COVID at ~65% of pre-pandemic visitor numbers. Fiordland Department of Conservation infrastructure constraints limit visitation growth. Regional unemployment remains near national average despite rural employment concentration.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
