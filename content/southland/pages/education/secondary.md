## Claims cited on this page

- **Early childhood education (ECE) accessibility in Southland is limited in rural areas. Invercargill has adequate licensed ECE providers (~12); rural and coastal areas (Te Anau, Orepuki, Catlins, Bluff) have sparse provision. Cost barriers and workforce shortages affect availability; low-income families face access constraints.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Tertiary education access in Southland is constrained by lack of local university provision. Southern Institute of Technology (now Te Pūkenga Southland campus) offers trades/vocational training; no undergraduate degree pathways locally. University study requires relocation to Dunedin (Otago University), Christchurch, or Auckland; financial and social barriers limit participation.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Tertiary participation in Southland shows declining local pathways. SIT's historic Zero Fees Scheme (pre-Te Pūkenga merger) attracted national students; post-merger model less distinctive. Otago Polytechnic/Te Pūkenga integration completed; Invercargill campus now primarily Level 4+ qualifications. Young people pursuing degree study largely depart region; retention post-graduation low.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Educational achievement in Southland lags national averages, with NCEA Level 2 attainment at 72% (Invercargill) and 68% in rural districts. University attendance rates are 45-50% of school leavers, with limited local tertiary options forcing young people to relocate to Dunedin, Christchurch, or Auckland.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.

- **ECE provision in Southland faces workforce supply constraints. Trained early childhood teachers remain scarce in rural areas; remuneration below Dunedin/Christchurch limits recruitment. Invercargill city ECE enrolment trends stable; rural district provision declining. Pre-school participation rates lag national average in remote communities.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Secondary school enrolment in Southland shows slight decline; rural school rolls diminishing. Brain drain to Invercargill/Dunedin/Christchurch for specialized programmes (advanced sciences, languages, arts); talented students depart for tertiary education and do not return. Post-school transition to local employment limited; university pathways require relocation.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Secondary education in Southland is concentrated in Invercargill. Southland Boys' College, Southland Girls' College, and James Hargest College (combined ~1,200 students) dominate; smaller provincial secondary schools (Gore High School, Winton Secondary) serve <200 students each. Limited specialist programme breadth outside Invercargill.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
