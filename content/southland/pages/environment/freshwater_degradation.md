## Claims cited on this page

- **Southland river quality improvement is slow despite regulatory efforts. Southland Water and Land Plan 2022 sets 2030 nitrogen reduction targets; uptake of riparian planting and nutrient management practices variable. Climate change (summer drying, high-flow events) adds stress. Indigenous species recovery (lamprey, native fish) hampered by habitat degradation. Recreational use (fishing, water sports) increasingly constrained by quality concerns.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Foveaux Strait coastal environment faces multiple stressors. Oyster fishery (dredge-managed, quota-limited) is economically significant; Bonamia parasite periodically affects stock. Shipping activity (log exports from Bluff, Fonterra product export) creates marine pollution risk. Inshore fish stocks under pressure; seal populations increasing, affecting fishery access.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Southland river quality degradation is widespread and measurable. Mataura River: nitrate 4-8mg/L (target <4); E. coli exceedances frequent. Oreti River: similar nitrogen loading. Waiau (Manapouri outlet): clearer but downstream degradation. Aparima: rural runoff pressures. Fish populations (native galaxias, banded kokopu) declining in degraded reaches.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Freshwater degradation in Southland is progressive. Dairy farm nitrogen excretion increasing; riparian strip adoption slow; municipal wastewater treatment (Invercargill) upgraded but small town systems aging. Southland Water and Land Plan 2022 targets phased improvement; industry adoption of best practice (nutrient budgets, rotational grazing) variable. Agricultural lobby resists stringent limits.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Foveaux Strait marine ecosystem is under sustained pressure. Oyster stock fluctuations (Bonamia risk); fishing effort management contested; shipping lane expansion proposed. Sea temperature rise affecting fish distribution; algal bloom frequency increasing. Maori customary fishery rights (Ngai Tahu) increasingly contested for sustainability vs livelihood balance.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Southland freshwater quality faces dairy farming pressures. Southland rivers (Mataura, Oreti, Waiau, Aparima) show elevated nitrate and phosphate levels from pastoral runoff. MfE Freshwater bottom lines (nitrogen, E. coli) breached in multiple catchments. Dairy intensification (Fonterra Edendale milk collection from 8,000+ farms) drives nutrient loading.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Fiordland ecosystem (Fiordland National Park 1.26M ha, Te Wāhipounamu UNESCO World Heritage) represents NZ's largest undisturbed wilderness. Rainforest (Milford Sound avg 7,000mm/year), alpine habitat, marine fjord ecosystem; endemic species (kea, yellow-eyed penguin, Fiordland crested penguin, marine mammals). Visitor pressure (~700,000 pre-COVID annually) creates infrastructure/conservation tension.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Fiordland ecosystem faces climate change and tourism pressure. Visitor recovery post-COVID to ~65% of pre-pandemic levels; infrastructure capacity (Milford Road, Te Anau accommodation) limiting growth. DOC funding constraints affecting track/hut maintenance. Pest control (stoats, rats) ongoing; marine invasive species risk from shipping. Rainfall intensification threatens infrastructure resilience.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
