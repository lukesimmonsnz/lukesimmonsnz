## Claims cited on this page

- **Digital infrastructure in rural Southland lags urban provision. Broadband coverage <95% in Catlins, Mataura Valley, western Te Anau; 4G mobile spotty in remote areas. Business and education hampered; telehealth/remote work capacity constrained. UFB (Ultra-Fast Broadband) rollout prioritized city areas; rural deployment extends through 2025-2026.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Manapouri Power Station is critical Southland and national energy infrastructure. Meridian Energy-owned; 850 MW capacity; underground cavern construction (largest in Southern Hemisphere when built 1969). Lake Manapouri level controversy (1960s-70s) pivotal for NZ environmental politics. Power supply dedicated to Tiwai Point smelter (supply agreement); inextricably linked.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Manapouri Power Station faces strategic uncertainty due to smelter dependency. Power contract extended to end 2026; post-2026 generation direction unconfirmed. Aging infrastructure (built 1969); refurbishment under way. Renewable energy policy unclear; hydro capacity available but no alternative major users committed. Meridian exploring battery storage/demand flexibility.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Digital infrastructure in rural Southland improving but lagging. UFB deployment ongoing; mobile network expansion constrained by profitability pressures. Work-from-home/remote education adoption limited by connectivity; rural enterprise digital capability below urban peers. Government rural broadband subsidy schemes supporting some community initiatives.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Water and wastewater infrastructure in Southland requires significant capital investment. Invercargill: water treatment upgrade project prioritized; wastewater treatment capacity constraints during high-flow events.
