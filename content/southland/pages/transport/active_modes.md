## Claims cited on this page

- **Active transport adoption in Southland shows slow growth. Invercargill city council investment in cycling lanes and walkways increasing; school bike-bus programs piloted. Rural cycling infrastructure remains minimal; topography and weather constraints limit adoption potential. E-bike uptake modest but growing.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Transport connectivity in Southland faces geographic challenges. SH1 spine (Invercargill-Gore-Balclutha); SH6 to Queenstown (Kingston route, scenic); SH99 to Catlins. Fiordland: Milford Road (SH94) single access (119km Queenstown to Milford, Homer Tunnel single-lane with traffic lights, avalanche closures 10-20 days/year). Stewart Island: ferry only (60min Bluff-Oban).** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Active transport mode share in Southland is 10-15% of commute journeys, concentrated in Invercargill (pop 46K). Rural areas and farming communities lack cycling infrastructure, forcing reliance on private vehicles for school/work commutes even short distances.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Fiordland access infrastructure under pressure. Visitor numbers recovering post-COVID; Milford Road capacity at peak during peak season (December-February). Homer Tunnel: 60-year-old single-lane tunnel; replacement discussed but cost (~$1B) prohibitive. DOC maintenance adequate but not growth-oriented. Te Anau accommodation capacity limiting visitor growth. Helicopter/small plane access alternative but expensive.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Invercargill City Council roading maintenance has deferred ~$15-20M of planned renewals due to funding pressures. Annual maintenance backlog growing 5-8% annually. Heavy vehicle usage (Fonterra milk tankers, log trucks to Bluff) accelerates deterioration on peripheral routes. Pothole repair reactive rather than preventive; asset condition index declining.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Fiordland access is constrained by Milford Road (SH94) bottleneck. Single road spine: 119km Queenstown to Milford Sound; Homer Tunnel single-lane with traffic lights (single direction 10-15min); avalanche risk closes road 10-20 days/year; weather delays frequent. No alternative road access. Pre-COVID ~700,000 annual Milford Sound visitors; post-COVID recovery to ~65%.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Transport connectivity in Southland faces infrastructure aging. SH1 Invercargill-Gore: state highway maintenance adequate; rural route deterioration emerging. Milford Road: DOC maintenance funded to preserve access but not expand capacity; Homer Tunnel replacement discussions ongoing (cost ~$1B). Fiordland visitor growth constrained by bottleneck. Public transport (bus) coverage limited outside Invercargill.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Invercargill roading network faces aging infrastructure. City arterials (Dee Street, Spey Street, Esk Street) show deteriorating surface with potholes and flooding during heavy rain.
