# Fiordland Highway Access and Bottlenecks

<p class="horizon-band">Analysis horizon: 10yr · 50yr</p>

### Fiordland SH94 Resilience and Alternative Routes

Investing in road maintenance and feasibility of alternative routes (e.g., Te Anau–Hollyford) improves access resilience.

**Flagship moves:**

- Accelerate SH94 maintenance and resilience improvements
- Evaluate alternative Fiordland access routes
- Emergency supply and visitor rerouting protocols

**Tensions:**

- High cost of alternative routes; limited ROI
- Environmental impact of new routes

**Interventions on the system:**

- **Fiordland access infrastructure under pressure. Visitor numbers recovering post-COVID; Milford Road capacity at peak during peak season (December-February). - **Invercargill City Council roading maintenance has deferred ~$15-20M of planned renewals due to funding pressures. Annual maintenance backlog growing 5-8% annually. Pothole repair reactive rather than preventive; asset condition index declining.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Fiordland access is constrained by Milford Road (SH94) bottleneck. Single road spine: 119km Queenstown to Milford Sound; Homer Tunnel single-lane with traffic lights (single direction 10-15min); avalanche risk closes road 10-20 days/year; weather delays frequent. No alternative road access. Pre-COVID ~700,000 annual Milford Sound visitors; post-COVID recovery to ~65%.** *(confidence: medium)* — Southland Regional Council Strategic Plan 2024.
- **Transport connectivity in Southland faces infrastructure aging. SH1 Invercargill-Gore: state highway maintenance adequate; rural route deterioration emerging.
