## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Crime prevention and early intervention

Evidence-based early intervention and community prevention programmes address the root causes of crime more effectively than enforcement alone.

**Flagship moves:**

- Fund Whānau Ora wraparound support in high-crime Hamilton suburbs
- Expand early childhood and youth at-risk programmes in deprived areas
- Implement whole-of-government social investment approach targeting known risk factors

**Tensions:**

- Prevention investment has delayed returns and is harder to measure than arrest statistics
- Community-led programmes require sustained resourcing that can be politically difficult

**Interventions on the system:**

- Fund Whānau Ora wraparound support in high-crime Hamilton suburbs (state variable: `recorded_crime_rate`, sign: +)


### Targeted enforcement and deterrence

Targeted policing of high-harm offending, particularly family violence and drug supply, reduces crime at lower cost than broad enforcement.

**Flagship moves:**

- Increase Hamilton Police family harm prevention units
- Expand drug rehabilitation courts as alternative to incarceration
- Implement place-based policing in high-crime Hamilton suburbs

**Tensions:**

- Enforcement focus without prevention investment addresses symptoms not causes
- Community-police trust is lower in Māori communities, reducing effectiveness

**Interventions on the system:**

- Increase Hamilton Police family harm prevention units (state variable: `recorded_crime_rate`, sign: +)
