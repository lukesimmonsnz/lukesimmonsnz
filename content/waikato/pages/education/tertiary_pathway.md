## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Community and whānau-centred education

Involving whānau and communities in education design improves engagement and attendance for Māori and Pasifika students.

**Flagship moves:**

- Fund community education workers in all high-deprivation Waikato schools
- Establish whānau education hubs linked to kura and mainstream schools
- Support marae-based learning programmes for disengaged rangatahi

**Tensions:**

- Community approaches require long-term investment before measurable gains
- Mainstream schools may resist community-led models

**Interventions on the system:**

- Fund community education workers in all high-deprivation Waikato schools (state variable: `ncea_attainment_rate`, sign: +)


### Equity-based school resourcing

Directing higher per-student funding to high-deprivation schools and kaupapa Māori education narrows achievement gaps.

**Flagship moves:**

- Increase equity funding weightings for decile 1-3 schools in Waikato
- Expand kura kaupapa Māori and bilingual unit provision
- Provide rural teacher incentives including housing support

**Tensions:**

- Higher funding does not automatically translate to achievement gains
- Rural incentives may increase teacher costs without improving outcomes

**Interventions on the system:**

- Increase equity funding weightings for decile 1-3 schools in Waikato (state variable: `ncea_attainment_rate`, sign: +)

## Claims cited on this page

- **Tertiary participation from provincial Waikato (South Waikato, Waitomo, Coromandel hinterland) is 38-42% versus Hamilton 50% plus. Cost of 20 to 25K annually and limited local pathways drive students to Auckland or Wellington, creating brain-drain from smaller towns. University of Waikato and Wintec offer Hamilton-based options but lack South Waikato satellite campuses.** *(confidence: medium)* — Ministry of Education Waikato Achievement Data 2023.
