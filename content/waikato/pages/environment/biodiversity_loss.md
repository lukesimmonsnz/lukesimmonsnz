## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Freshwater limit setting and enforcement

Strong regulatory freshwater limits and enforcement of the NPSFM are necessary to halt degradation.

**Flagship moves:**

- Set binding nitrate caps for all major Waikato River sub-catchments
- Increase WRC monitoring stations and enforcement capability
- Require riparian fencing on all intensively farmed land by 2027

**Tensions:**

- Regulatory approach may reduce farm profitability and trigger rural political opposition
- Enforcement capacity is limited relative to the scale of non-compliance

**Interventions on the system:**

- Set binding nitrate caps for all major Waikato River sub-catchments (state variable: `environmental_quality_index`, sign: +)


### Regenerative and restorative farming transition

Transitioning to lower-intensity and regenerative farming practices reduces contaminant loads while maintaining rural livelihoods.

**Flagship moves:**

- Fund transition to lower-input farming systems via co-investment fund
- Expand riparian planting programmes with Te Awa Tupua
- Provide incentives for organic conversion in critical catchments

**Tensions:**

- Voluntary transition is too slow without regulatory backstop
- Economic viability of low-intensity farming varies by soil type and market access

**Interventions on the system:**

- Fund transition to lower-input farming systems via co-investment fund (state variable: `environmental_quality_index`, sign: +)

- **Waikato wetlands have declined approximately 90% since 1840 due to agricultural drainage and land conversion. The Waikato Lowlands originally hosted extensive wetland complexes; surviving remnants at Whangamarino and Kopuatai are now DOC-managed reserves. Peat bog loss in the central Waikato plains reduced carbon sequestration capacity and increased flood severity.** *(confidence: medium)* — NIWA Waikato Climate Projections 2024.
