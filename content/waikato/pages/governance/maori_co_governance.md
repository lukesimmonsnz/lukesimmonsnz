## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Territorial authority rationalisation

Merging smaller Waikato TAs reduces duplication and builds strategic planning capacity.

**Flagship moves:**

- Commission independent review of Waikato TA boundaries and roles
- Pilot shared services for consenting, infrastructure, and planning
- Strengthen WRC's regional spatial planning role

**Tensions:**

- Amalgamation faces strong community resistance and democratic legitimacy concerns
- Merged councils may lose local responsiveness

**Interventions on the system:**

- Commission independent review of Waikato TA boundaries and roles (state variable: `governance_effectiveness`, sign: +)


### Treaty-based co-governance strengthening

Strengthening Waikato-Tainui and Tainui co-governance roles improves legitimacy and environmental outcomes.

**Flagship moves:**

- Implement full Te Awa Tupua governance framework across all river management decisions
- Require iwi participation in all major regional consenting processes
- Resource Waikato-Tainui governance capacity to meet co-governance obligations

**Tensions:**

- Co-governance can slow decision-making if processes are not well-designed
- Resource requirements for co-governance may strain smaller council budgets

**Interventions on the system:**

- Implement full Te Awa Tupua governance framework across all river management decisions (state variable: `governance_effectiveness`, sign: +)

## Claims cited on this page

- **Waikato-Tainui co-governance through the Waikato River Authority represents a nationally significant model: 50% Waikato Regional Council and 50% Waikato-Tainui under the 2010 Settlement Act. However, WRA budget constraints and staffing gaps limit implementation of comprehensive river management and cultural restoration. Te Awa Tupua (Whanganui River personhood) precedent was not applied to Waikato despite iwi advocacy.** *(confidence: medium)* — Waikato-Tainui Environmental Management Report 2023.
