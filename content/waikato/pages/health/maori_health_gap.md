## Māori health outcomes gap

## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Kaupapa Māori health service expansion

Expanding kaupapa Māori health providers and community health workers reduces inequity through culturally appropriate care.

**Flagship moves:**

- Fund 15 additional Māori health providers in the Waikato region
- Expand community health workers in high-deprivation areas
- Integrate whānau ora into primary care pathways

**Tensions:**

- Kaupapa Māori health requires sustained workforce development pipeline
- Integration with mainstream health system requires institutional change

**Interventions on the system:**

- Fund 15 additional Māori health providers in the Waikato region (state variable: `health_outcome_index`, sign: +)


### Primary care access improvement

Expanding primary care access, particularly in rural areas, reduces avoidable hospitalisations.

**Flagship moves:**

- Establish three new community health centres in rural Waikato
- Expand telehealth for routine specialist consultations
- Recruit and retain rural health workers through incentive scheme

**Tensions:**

- Telehealth cannot fully substitute for in-person care for complex needs
- Rural recruitment incentives create equity concerns with urban areas

**Interventions on the system:**

- Establish three new community health centres in rural Waikato (state variable: `health_outcome_index`, sign: +)

## Claims cited on this page

- **Waikato Māori life expectancy is approximately 7 years below the regional non-Māori average (71 versus 78 years). Excess mortality in cardiovascular disease, respiratory illness, and diabetes reflects occupational exposure (dairy farming, forestry), health service access barriers, and socioeconomic stress. Waikato DHB Midlands Hauora partnership addresses equity but faces chronic underfunding relative to Māori population proportion.** *(confidence: medium)* — Health New Zealand Waikato District 2023.
