## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Community Safety and Alcohol/Drug Harm Reduction

West Coast's high alcohol and methamphetamine use drives family violence and crime; community-based harm reduction programmes are more effective than enforcement alone.

**Flagship moves:**

- Fund West Coast alcohol and drug harm reduction programmes
- Expand community outreach for family violence prevention in isolated areas
- Establish youth diversion programme with Ngāi Tahu partnership

**Tensions:**

- Small communities lack anonymity, reducing help-seeking for drug and family violence issues
- Resource constraints limit both enforcement and support service capacity

**Interventions on the system:**

- Establish West Coast Drug and Alcohol Community Support Hub (state variable: `drug_harm_index`, sign: -)


### Rural Safety Network and First Responder Capacity

Extreme geographic isolation means emergency response times in West Coast are among the longest in NZ; volunteer networks and rural safety coordination are critical.

**Flagship moves:**

- Strengthen St John and fire service volunteer capacity in isolated West Coast communities
- Develop rural safety communication network for Haast, Karamea, and Reefton
- Fund rural police community liaison officers

**Tensions:**

- Volunteer retention is difficult in depopulating communities
- Long response times are a structural consequence of geography that funding cannot fully overcome

**Interventions on the system:**

- Develop rural emergency response network for isolated communities (state variable: `emergency_response_time`, sign: -)
