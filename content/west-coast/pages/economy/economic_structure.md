## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Mining Transition and Green Economy Development

Coal mining decline is inevitable; West Coast needs proactive just-transition investment to develop alternative economic sectors including renewable energy, conservation economy, and value-add food production.

**Flagship moves:**

- Establish West Coast Just Transition Fund from Crown-Māori partnership
- Develop pumped hydro or green hydrogen facility leveraging existing generation capacity
- Support conservation tourism economy through DOC partnership

**Tensions:**

- Mining communities have deep cultural attachment to the industry beyond economics
- Green economy development requires sustained long-term investment before employment returns

**Interventions on the system:**

- Create West Coast Economic Diversification Fund targeting 200 new jobs by 2030 (state variable: `employment_outside_mining`, sign: +)


### Tourism Resilience and High-Value Visitor Economy

West Coast tourism is volatile and dependent on international visitors; building a year-round, high-value domestic and international tourism economy improves economic resilience.

**Flagship moves:**

- Invest in all-weather tourism infrastructure (Punakaiki visitor centre, Hokitika waterfront)
- Develop Great Walk extensions and new multi-day tracks
- Partner with Poutini Ngāi Tahu to develop culturally-anchored tourism experiences

**Tensions:**

- Tourism infrastructure investment requires central government co-funding in a low-revenue region
- Over-reliance on tourism recreates a new form of single-sector dependency

**Interventions on the system:**

- Develop West Coast high-value tourism strategy with Poutini Ngāi Tahu (state variable: `tourism_yield_per_visitor`, sign: +)
