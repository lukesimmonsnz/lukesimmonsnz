## Solution camps

A number of distinct positions recur in policy debates on this issue. Each is defensible on its own terms; none is obviously correct. Presented in alphabetical order without ranking.


### Small School Sustainability and Digital Learning

West Coast's very small rural schools face closure pressure; digital learning hubs and multi-school clusters can sustain educational provision without large-scale consolidation.

**Flagship moves:**

- Establish digital learning hub network across West Coast small schools
- Fund itinerant specialist teachers (arts, STEM, te reo Māori) across school clusters
- Provide high-quality fibre to all West Coast schools

**Tensions:**

- Digital learning cannot fully replace in-person specialist teaching
- Some families will still relocate for larger school options regardless of quality improvements

**Interventions on the system:**

- Fund digital learning cluster programme for West Coast small schools (state variable: `student_achievement_ncea`, sign: +)


### Tertiary Pathways and Vocational Training

West Coast lacks local tertiary provision; building vocational training pipelines for trades, mining transition skills, and conservation management supports economic diversification.

**Flagship moves:**

- Establish West Coast trades training hub in Greymouth linked to Ara Institute
- Fund NZ Conservation Corps programme creating pathways for rangatahi into conservation work
- Expand WEL Networks digital skills programme

**Tensions:**

- Small regional population limits viability of local tertiary provision
- Brain drain accelerates when local youth acquire qualifications and leave for larger centres

**Interventions on the system:**

- Establish West Coast Vocational Training Hub in Greymouth (state variable: `regional_qualification_attainment`, sign: +)
