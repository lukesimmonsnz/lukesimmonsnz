### Marine Protected Areas and Ecosystem-Based Management

West Coast's unique coastal and marine ecosystem requires formal protection and integrated management to sustain fisheries, biodiversity, and tourism.

**Flagship moves:**

- Establish West Coast Marine Protected Area network covering key reef and estuarine habitats
- Develop integrated Westland coastal management plan with Poutini Ngāi Tahu
- Enforce commercial fishing limits in sensitive inshore habitats

**Tensions:**

- Fishing industry opposes MPAs that restrict commercial access
- Enforcement capacity on a remote coastline is very limited

## Claims cited on this page

- **Sea level on New Zealand's West Coast has risen approximately 1.6 mm per year over the past century, with projections of 0.5-1.0 m by 2100. West Coast settlements at sea level (Westport, Greymouth, Hokitika) face inundation and saltwater intrusion into freshwater aquifers. Combined with Alpine Fault risk and high rainfall flooding, coastal communities face compounded climate and seismic hazards.** [value: 1.6 mm/year historical rise; 1920-2024] — West Coast Regional Council Annual Plan 2024; Westland / Tai Poutini National Park Management Plan 2023.
