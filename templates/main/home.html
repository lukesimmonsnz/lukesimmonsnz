About

Hello.

I’m Luke. I live in Auckland, studying AI and software engineering and working toward a career as an AI engineer. Most of my time goes into building local-first systems — agents and tools that run on my own hardware rather than in the cloud.

Before this I spent fifteen years as a live-sound audio engineer, mostly volunteering with churches around Auckland. Elsewhere online I go by nzgamerluke.

The other half of this site is an archive of my grandfather, David Roy Simmons (Māori name Rawiri Te Puru Terehou) — ethnologist, author of The Great New Zealand Myth, and co-curator of the Te Maori exhibition. I’m building it because his work deserves to be easier to find.

About this site

Built with Flask and Jinja, styled by hand in one stylesheet, served locally on my own machine. No tracking, no analytics, no third-party scripts. Set in Fraunces for display and Inter for body text. The cover photograph was taken in Aotearoa.

I’m studying AI, so it felt right to build with it. Anthropic’s Claude (via Claude Code) did a lot of the scaffolding, template wiring, and copy-editing alongside me. The editorial decisions — what goes in, what it says, what it looks like — are mine.
