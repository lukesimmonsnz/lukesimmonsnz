How these are made

Two authorship regimes

Not all four branches are made the same way. I want that to be obvious rather than buried in a disclosure at the bottom of the page, because it changes how much weight you should give any particular claim.

agent

Computer Science, Climate Science & AI, Medical Science

These three branches are AI-drafted reference notes I commissioned for my own study and publish unedited, in case they’re useful to someone moving through similar material. Treat them as machine-written study notes — useful for orientation, not authoritative; verify any claim before citing.

agent + luke

Auckland

The Auckland branch is AI-drafted in iterative dialogue with me — I read the material and work through it with the agent by question and answer, shaping framing and scope. Every claim cites a primary source via a lint-gated entity graph, which is a structural check — independent per-claim fact-checking by me is a step that should sit between drafting and publication and is not yet in place.

Both regimes are a deliberate choice, not an accident. The first gets me reading material faster than I could write it myself. The second applies editorial discipline where the subject actually needs it. Neither set of pages is peer-reviewed scholarship or original research.
